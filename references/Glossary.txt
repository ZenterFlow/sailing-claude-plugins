# Sailing Terms Glossary

Complete definitions for nautical and sailing terminology.

---

## **A**
**Abaft**: Toward or at the stern of a vessel; behind.
**Abeam**: At a right angle to the length of the ship, on either side.
**Aboard**: On or in a vessel.
**About**: On the opposite tack (e.g., "tack about").
**Abandon**: To leave a vessel, typically due to emergency.
**Adrift**: Floating without being moored or steered; loose at sea.
**Aft**: At or toward the stern (rear) of a vessel.
**Aground**: When the hull has touched the bottom and is stuck.
**Ahead**: In front of the vessel; forward direction.
**Air Draught**: The vertical distance from waterline to the highest point on the vessel.
**Aloft**: Up in the rigging or mast; above the deck.
**Amidships**: In the middle portion of the vessel.
**Anchor**: A heavy device attached to chain/rope that holds a vessel to the seabed.
**Anchor Ball**: A black circular shape displayed by a vessel at anchor during daylight.
**Anchorage**: A suitable place for anchoring; designated anchoring area.
**Anchoring**: The act of deploying an anchor to secure a vessel.
**Anchor Chain**: Heavy chain connecting anchor to vessel.
**Anemometer**: Instrument measuring wind speed.
**Anti Fouling**: Protective coating on hull to prevent marine growth.
**Apparent Wind**: The wind experienced on a moving vessel (true wind + vessel motion).
**Astern**: Behind the vessel; backward direction.
**Astro Navigation**: Position fixing using celestial bodies.
**Autopilot**: Self-steering system for vessels.

---

## **B**
**Back**: To turn the sail/backwind it; to row backward.
**Backing**: Wind shifting counter-clockwise (or direction opposite to veering).
**Backstay**: Stay supporting the mast from aft, preventing forward movement.
**Baggywrinkle**: Chafe protection made of old rope strands on rigging.
**Bail**: To remove water from a vessel; also a bail (handle).
**Ballast**: Weight in the hull for stability (often lead or iron).
**Bar**: Shallow sandbank or obstruction; also a sandbar.
**Bar - Pub**: Establishment serving drinks (shore term).
**Batten**: Flexible strip in sail leech to maintain shape.
**Beam or Abeam**: The width of a vessel; at right angles to centerline.
**Beat or Beating**: Sailing upwind by tacking back and forth.
**Beaufort Force**: Wind speed scale from 0-12.
**Berth**: Sleeping quarters; also a mooring place.
**Bilge/Bilge Pump**: Lowest interior part of hull; pump to remove water.
**Bitter End**: The very end of an anchor cable.
**Block**: Pulley system for rope.
**Bollard**: Strong post on quay for mooring lines.
**Boltrope**: Rope sewn into sail edge for reinforcement.
**Boom**: Horizontal spar supporting foot of sail.
**Boom - Floating**: Floating barrier to contain oil spills.
**Bottlescrew**: Tensioning device for rigging (turnbuckle).
**Bow**: Front of the vessel.
**Bowthruster**: Lateral propulsion device at bow for maneuvering.
**Bowline**: Type of knot forming a fixed loop; also a headsail type.
**Bowsprit**: Spar extending forward from bow for headsails.
**Breakwater**: Structure protecting harbor from waves.
**Broach**: When vessel turns uncontrollably broadside to waves/wind.
**Bulkhead**: Interior wall/partition in vessel.
**Bumpkin**: Short spar projecting from stern for sheets.
**Buoy**: Floating navigation mark.

---

## **C**
**Cable**: Heavy anchor rope/chain; also nautical unit of distance (0.1 nm).
**Cannard**: Forward-mounted foil or sail configuration (canard).
**Capsize**: To overturn in water.
**Capstan**: Vertical winch for hauling anchor/heavy lines.
**Catamaran**: Twin-hulled vessel.
**Centreboard**: Retractable keel for lateral resistance.
**Chain Locker**: Storage compartment for anchor chain.
**Chart Datum**: Reference level for chart soundings (usually lowest tide).
**Chronometer**: Highly accurate timepiece for navigation.
**Clew**: Lower aft corner of a sail.
**Close-Hauled**: Sailing as close to the wind as possible.
**Cockpit**: Recessed area for steering and crew.
**Companionway**: Steps/ladder leading below deck.
**Compass Fluxgate**: Electronic compass sensor.
**Compass - Handheld**: Portable magnetic compass.
**Compass - Ships**: Main vessel compass.
**Convergence**: Meeting of air masses; also lines meeting on chart.
**Cringle**: Reinforced eyelet in sail for attaching lines.
**Crosstrees**: Horizontal supports near masthead.
**Cunningham**: Tensioning line to adjust sail shape at luff.

---

## **D**
**Danbuoy**: Emergency marker buoy with flag and light.
**Daggerboard**: Retractable centreboard that lifts vertically.
**Davit**: Crane for launching lifeboats/dinghies.
**Dead ahead**: Directly forward.
**Dead Reckoning (DR)**: Position calculated from course and speed.
**Dead run**: Sailing directly downwind.
**Decks**: Horizontal surfaces/platforms of vessel.
**Derrick**: Hoisting device/crane.
**Deviation**: Compass error caused by vessel's magnetic fields.
**Differential GPS**: Enhanced GPS accuracy using reference stations.
**Dinghy**: Small boat, often tender or sailing dinghy.
**Directional Light**: Navigation light with specific arc.
**Divergence**: Separating of air masses; lines diverging on chart.
**Direction of Buoyage**: Indicated direction for safe navigation.
**Displacement**: Weight of water displaced by hull; hull shape.
**Dock**: Enclosed water area for mooring; also the act of mooring.
**Dodger**: Canvas screen protecting cockpit from spray.
**Dog Watch**: 2-hour watch (1600-1800 or 1800-2000).
**Doldrums**: Calm equatorial region with light winds.
**Dolphin**: Cluster of piles for mooring.
**Douglas Protractor**: Navigation plotting tool.
**Downhaul**: Line to tension downward (e.g., on sail or spar).
**Draught**: Depth of hull below waterline.
**Drogue**: Sea anchor dragged to slow drift.

---

## **E**
**Ebb Tide**: Tide going out, water level falling.
**ECDIS**: Electronic Chart Display and Information System.
**Echo Sounder**: Device measuring water depth using sound.
**EGNOS**: European Geostationary Navigation Overlay Service (GPS augmentation).
**Engine**: Propulsion machinery.
**Ensign**: National flag flown on vessel.
**EPIRB**: Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacon.
**Eye splice**: Permanent loop in rope end.

---

## **F**
**Fairway**: Navigable channel.
**Fairlead**: Guide for rope to reduce chafe.
**Fender**: Protective cushion between vessel and dock/other vessel.
**Fetch**: Distance wind travels over water; also to bring in a line.
**Fid**: Tool for splicing rope.
**Fix (position)**: Determined position of vessel.
**Flake**: To lay rope in overlapping coils.
**Flank**: To sail too close to wind (pinching).
**Flares**: Emergency signaling pyrotechnics.
**Flood Tide**: Tide coming in, water level rising.
**Fluke**: Holding part of anchor.
**Folding Propeller**: Prop with blades that fold when not in use.
**Following Sea**: Waves from astern.
**Foot**: Lower edge of sail.
**Force**: Strength (wind, etc.).
**Fore Forward**: Toward the bow.
**Forecastle (fo'c'sle)**: Forward crew quarters.
**Forestay**: Stay supporting mast from forward.
**Foul**: Entangled or obstructed.
**Foulies**: Waterproof clothing (oilskins).
**Freeboard**: Height of deck above waterline.
**Full and By**: Sailing close-hauled with sails full.
**Furl**: To roll or fold sail.

---

## **G**
**Gaff rig**: Rig with four-sided mainsail on spar (gaff).
**Galley**: Kitchen area on vessel.
**Gangplank**: Board for boarding from dock.
**Gangway**: Passage; also boarding ramp.
**Gennaker**: Large lightweight headsail for reaching/downwind.
**Genoa**: Large overlapping headsail.
**Gimble**: Pivoting mount keeping object level.
**Give Way**: Vessel required to yield (ColRegs).
**Glass**: Barometer; also telescope.
**GPS**: Global Positioning System.
**Going about**: Tacking (changing direction through wind).
**Gooseneck**: Fitting attaching boom to mast.
**Goosewinged**: Sailing downwind with jib opposite mainsail.
**Grounding**: Vessel touching bottom.
**Gybe**: Turning stern through wind (downwind turn).

---

## **H**
**Halyard**: Line for raising/lowering sails.
**Hammock**: Canvas bed, often suspended.
**Handy billy**: Portable tackle for various uses.
**Hank**: Clip attaching headsail to stay.
**Harbour**: Sheltered body of water for mooring.
**Hard**: Become steady (e.g., "hard alee").
**Harden up**: To trim sails closer to wind.
**HAT**: Highest Astronomical Tide.
**HDOP**: Horizontal Dilution of Precision (GPS accuracy).
**Head heads**: Toilet; also top of sail.
**Headsail**: Sail forward of mast (jib, genoa).
**Heaving To**: Setting sails to maintain position with minimal movement.
**Heeling**: Leaning over due to wind pressure.
**Helm Helmsman**: Steering position/person steering.
**Hitch**: Knot for attaching to a ring or post.
**HOT**: Head-on traffic (radar/navigation).
**Hounds**: Attachment points for shrouds on mast.
**Hull**: Main body of the vessel.
**Hull speed**: Theoretical maximum speed based on waterline length.
**Hydrofoil**: Wing-like structure lifting hull out of water at speed.

---

## **I**
**IALA-A**: International Association of Lighthouse Authorities Region A (red port hand marks).
**IALA-B**: IALA Region B (red starboard hand marks).
**In Irons**: Stalled facing into wind, unable to tack.
**Icing**: Ice buildup on vessel.
**Inboard Engine**: Engine mounted inside hull.
**Inboard - Outboard**: Sterndrive system.

---

## **J**
**Jackstays**: Safety lines along deck for harness attachment.
**Jammers**: Rope holding cleats.
**Jetsam**: Goods thrown overboard.
**Jetty**: Pier or structure projecting into water.
**Jib**: Triangular headsail.
**Jigger-mast**: After mast on ketch/yawl.
**Junk**: Old rope; also Chinese sailing vessel type.
**Jury rig**: Temporary/emergency rigging.

---

## **K**
**Kedge**: Secondary anchor; also to warp vessel using anchor.
**Keel**: Main structural element along bottom for stability.
**Ketch**: Two-masted vessel with mizzen aft of mainmast.
**Kicker**: Boom vang (downward tension on boom).
**Kill cord**: Safety cord that cuts engine if detached.
**Knockdown**: Extreme heeling, often to horizontal.
**Knot**: Speed unit (1 nautical mile per hour); also a tying method.

---

## **L**
**Laid up**: Vessel out of commission.
**Lanby**: Large automatic navigation buoy.
**Land lubber**: Person unfamiliar with sea.
**Lanyard**: Short cord for securing items.
**LAT**: Lowest Astronomical Tide.
**Lateral (mark)**: Navigation mark indicating safe side of channel.
**Latitude**: Angular distance north/south of equator.
**Launch**: To put vessel in water; also a large boat.
**Lazaret**: Storage locker at stern.
**Lazyjacks**: Lines to contain sail when lowered.
**Lee cloth**: Cloth preventing falling from berth.
**Lee shore**: Shore toward which wind blows.
**Leeboard**: Pivoting board on lee side for shallow draft vessels.
**Leech**: After edge of sail.
**Lee-oh**: Command to tack.
**Leeward**: Downwind side.
**Leeway**: Sideways drift due to wind.
**Length Overall (LOA)**: Total length of vessel.
**Life jacket**: Personal flotation device.
**Liferaft**: Emergency inflatable raft.
**Life ring**: Buoyant ring for rescue.
**Line**: Rope on board vessel.
**Loose footed**: Sail not attached to boom along foot.
**Luff**: Forward edge of sail; also to sail too close to wind.
**Luffing up**: Turning toward wind.

---

## **M**
**Magnetic Compass**: Compass using Earth's magnetic field.
**Magnitude**: Brightness of celestial body.
**Mainmast**: Principal mast.
**Mainsail**: Principal sail on mainmast.
**Mainsheet**: Control line for mainsail angle.
**Make fast**: To secure a line.
**Making way**: Vessel moving through water.
**Man Overboard**: Emergency when person falls in water.
**Marina**: Berthing facility with services.
**Marlinspike**: Tool for rope work.
**Mast**: Vertical spar supporting sails.
**Masthead rig**: Forestay attached at mast top.
**Mayday**: Distress call.
**Mercator projection**: Chart projection method.
**Meridian**: Line of longitude.
**Minor / Major light**: Lighthouse classification.
**Mizzen**: After mast on ketch/yawl; also its sail.
**Moire light**: Light with special characteristic (distinguishing pattern).
**Mole**: Massive breakwater structure.
**Monkey fist**: Weighted knot in heaving line.
**Moor**: To secure vessel to anchor or buoys.
**Morse Code**: Signal code using dots/dashes.
**Morse Control**: Engine throttle/gear lever system.

---

## **N**
**Nautical Almanac**: Tables for celestial navigation.
**Nautical Mile**: 1,852 meters (approximately 1.15 statute miles).
**Nautical Twilight**: Period when sun is 6-12° below horizon.
**Navigable**: Safe for navigation.
**Navigable Semi-Circle**: Safer part of tropical storm to navigate.
**Navigation Lights**: Required lights for vessel identification at night.
**Neaps**: Neap tides with lower range (smaller tidal variation).

---

## **O**
**Oar**: Pole for rowing.
**Offshore (wind)**: Wind blowing from land to sea.
**Off the Wind**: Sailing downwind.
**Oilies or Oilskins**: Waterproof protective clothing.
**Onshore (wind)**: Wind blowing from sea to land.
**Outboard**: External motor; also toward the side of vessel.
**Outdrive (Inboard-Outboard)**: Stern drive unit combining engine and propeller.
**Outhaul**: Tensioning line for sail foot.
**Overboard**: Over the side into water.
**Overfalls**: Turbulent water over shallow area or tide race.

---

## **P**
**Paddle**: Short oar; also to propel by hand.
**Painter**: Towing line on dinghy.
**Palm**: Leather pad for sewing canvas/sails.
**Parallel rule**: Navigation tool for plotting courses.
**Pay off**: To let out line; also vessel falling away from wind.
**Pennant**: Triangular flag.
**Phonetic alphabet**: Spelling alphabet (Alpha, Bravo, Charlie...).
**Pier**: Structure projecting into water for mooring.
**Pile Mooring**: Mooring to driven piles in seabed.
**Pilot**: Local navigation expert; also guidance system.
**Pilotage**: Navigation by visual references.
**Pilothouse**: Enclosed steering position.
**Pinch**: To sail too close to wind (flanking).
**Pitch**: Bow up/down motion; also propeller pitch.
**Plane**: To skim over water at high speed (hydroplaning).
**Plotter**: Navigation instrument; also person plotting.
**Point up**: To sail closer to wind direction.
**Points of sail**: Different sailing angles relative to wind direction.
**Polaris**: North Star.
**Polars**: Performance data for vessel speed at various wind angles.
**Pontoon**: Floating platform; also buoyancy tube on RIB.
**Port**: Left side of vessel; harbor; also fortified wine.
**Port of entry**: Official customs entry point for vessels.
**Port Hand Lateral Mark**: Red buoy/mark (IALA A) indicating port side of channel.
**Port tack**: Sailing with wind from port side (starboard side to wind).
**Porthole**: Window in hull/superstructure.
**Preferred Channel Mark**: Buoy indicating main channel at junction.
**Preventer**: Line preventing accidental gybe.
**Propeller**: Rotating device propelling vessel through water.
**Prop walk**: Sideways thrust from propeller when in gear.
**Pulpit**: Safety rail at bow.
**Purse seine**: Fishing net method using drawstring closure.

---

## **Q**
**Quadrant**: 90° arc; instrument for measuring celestial altitude.
**Quarter**: Side of vessel between beam and stern.
**Quarter berth**: Bunk located at quarter of vessel.
**Quarter deck**: Aft deck area traditionally for officers.
**Quay**: Solid structure parallel to shore for mooring.

---

## **R**
**RACON**: Radar transponder beacon.
**RADAR**: Radio Detection and Ranging system.
**RADAR reflector**: Device to enhance vessel's radar return.
**Rafting**: Mooring vessels side by side.
**Reach**: Sailing across wind (beam reach, close reach, broad reach).
**Ready About**: Command before tacking ("Ready about").
**Reciprocal**: Opposite direction (180° from current course).
**Reef**: To reduce sail area; also shallow underwater area.
**Reefing pennants**: Lines for securing reefed sail.
**Reef knot**: Square knot for joining two lines.
**Reeve**: To pass rope through a block or eye.
**Relative bearing**: Bearing relative to vessel's heading.
**Rhumb line**: Constant compass course line crossing all meridians at same angle.
**RIB**: Rigid Inflatable Boat.
**Riding turns**: Turns of rope on winch drum.
**Rigging**: All ropes and cables supporting masts and sails.
**Roach**: Curved leech of sail extending beyond straight line.
**Roller reefing**: System for rolling sail around boom or stay.
**Round up**: To turn into wind; also to gather crew.
**Rudder**: Underwater steering surface.
**Running**: Sailing downwind.
**Running backstays**: Adjustable stays for mast support when running.
**Running fix**: Position from multiple bearing lines taken at different times.
**Running rigging**: Adjustable lines (halyards, sheets) for sail control.

---

## **S**
**Safewater**: Navigable water; also center-channel mark (red/white).
**Sail**: Fabric device for capturing wind for propulsion.
**Sailing free**: Sailing with wind aft (not close-hauled).
**Sail drive**: Engine drive system with integrated saildrive leg.
**Sail makers needle**: Large triangular needle for canvas work.
**Sail makers palm**: Leather sewing pad worn on hand.
**Saloon**: Main cabin/living area below deck.
**Schooner**: Multi-masted vessel with forward mast shorter or same height.
**Screw**: Propeller; also threaded fastener.
**Sea anchor**: Drogue deployed from bow to stabilize vessel.
**Sea breeze**: Onshore wind from land/sea temperature differential.
**Seacock**: Valve for underwater through-hull fitting.
**Sea level**: Average ocean surface height reference.
**Self bailing**: Drains automatically by gravity (e.g., cockpit).
**Self righting**: Ability to return upright after capsizing.
**Semidiurnal**: Twice daily (tides).
**Sextant**: Instrument measuring angle between celestial objects and horizon.
**Shackle**: U-shaped connector secured with pin.
**Shaft Drive**: Propeller shaft directly from engine.
**Sheets**: Control lines for adjusting sail angle.
**Sheet bend**: Knot for joining two lines of different thickness.
**Ship's log**: Device measuring speed/distance through water; also official record.
**Shoal**: Shallow underwater area; also group of fish.

---

## **T**
**Tachometer**: Engine RPM gauge.
**Tack**: To change direction through wind; also lower forward corner of sail; also course relative to wind.
**Tailwind**: Wind from astern.
**Telltale**: Streamer showing wind/air flow direction.
**Tender**: Small boat for transport to shore.
**Territorial waters**: Waters under national jurisdiction (typically 12 nm).
**Throttle**: Engine speed control.
**Tidal Diamond**: Chart symbol giving tidal stream data for specific location.
**Tide**: Vertical water movement due to celestial bodies.
**Tidal Gate**: Constricted area with strong tidal flow.
**Tiller**: Lever arm for steering (attached to rudder head).
**Toerail**: Rail at deck edge for footing and strength.
**Topgallant**: Mast section above topsail; also sail flown there.
**Topping Lift (topper)**: Line supporting boom when mainsail lowered.
**Topsail**: Sail above lower sail (e.g., above mainsail).
**Tow**: To pull another vessel.
**Track**: Course over ground; also groove for sail slide.
**Tradewinds**: Reliable easterly winds in tropics.
**Traffic Separation**: Shipping lanes separating opposing traffic.
**Transit**: Passing; also pair of marks forming line of sight.
**Transom**: Flat stern surface of vessel.
**Traveller**: Adjustable slide for sheet block on track.
**Trawler**: Fishing vessel dragging nets.
**Trim**: Adjust sail angle; also vessel attitude in water fore/aft.

---

## **U**
**Under sail**: Propelled by sails.
**Under way**: Vessel moving through water (not anchored or aground).
**UTC (Universal time)**: Coordinated Universal Time (formerly GMT).
**Upper shrouds**: Rigging from masthead to sides of vessel.
**Up wind**: Toward wind direction (windward).

---

## **V**
**Vang**: Device pulling boom down (boom vang/kicker).
**Vanishing Angle Stability**: Angle of heel at which vessel capsizes.
**Variation**: Difference between magnetic and true north.
**V berth**: V-shaped berth in bow of vessel.
**Vector**: Quantity with direction and magnitude (e.g., wind, current).
**Veer**: Wind shifting clockwise; also to pay out rope/anchor.
**Very Light (Very Pistol)**: Flare gun for distress signals.
**VHF**: Very High Frequency radio (marine communication).
**VMG**: Velocity Made Good (progress toward destination).

---

## **W**
**WAAS**: Wide Area Augmentation System (GPS accuracy enhancement).
**Wake**: Disturbed water behind moving vessel.
**Wash**: Waves from vessel's propeller or hull.
**Warp**: To move vessel by lines; also rope used for this purpose.
**Watch**: Duty period for crew (typically 4 hours).
**Waterline**: Where hull meets water surface.
**Waypoint**: Navigational coordinate in planned route.
**Weigh anchor**: To raise anchor.
**Wharf**: Structure for mooring alongside.
**Wheel**: Steering wheel connected to rudder.
**Whipping**: Binding rope end to prevent fraying.
**White horses**: Whitecaps on wave crests.
**Winch**: Mechanical device for hauling lines under load.
**Windex**: Wind direction indicator at masthead.
**Windlass**: Mechanical device for raising anchor.
**Wind vane**: Device showing wind direction.
**Windward**: Toward wind (upwind side).

---

## **X**
**XTE**: Cross Track Error (deviation from planned course).

---

## **Y**
**Yacht**: Recreational sailing or motor vessel.
**Yankee**: Headsail type (between jib and genoa in size).
**Yaw**: Deviating off course side-to-side.
**Yawl**: Two-masted vessel with mizzen aft of rudderpost.
**Yoke**: Steering connection; also control handle.

---

## **Z**
**Zodiac**: Inflatable boat (often refers to brand/type).
