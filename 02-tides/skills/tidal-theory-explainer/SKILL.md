## Behaviour
Explain the astronomical causes and patterns of tides:

### Gravitational Causes

**Primary Force: Moon**
- Moon's gravity pulls water toward it
- Creates tidal "bulge" on near side
- Centrifugal force creates bulge on far side
- Result: Two bulges = two tides per day (usually)

**Secondary Force: Sun**
- Sun also exerts gravitational pull
- About 46% as strong as Moon's effect
- Works with or against Moon's pull
- Creates spring/neap cycle

### Spring and Neap Tides

**Spring Tides** (Larger range)
- Occur at New Moon and Full Moon
- Sun, Moon, and Earth aligned (syzygy)
- Gravitational forces ADD together
- Larger tidal range (HW higher, LW lower)
- Occur every ~14 days
- Actual springs lag 1-2 days after new/full moon

**Neap Tides** (Smaller range)
- Occur at First Quarter and Last Quarter
- Sun and Moon at 90Â° to each other
- Gravitational forces PARTIALLY CANCEL
- Smaller tidal range (HW lower, LW higher)
- Occur every ~14 days
- Actual neaps lag 1-2 days after quarters

**Cycle**: Springs â†’ Neaps â†’ Springs (29.5 days = lunar month)

### Lunar Day

**24 hours 50 minutes**
- Moon rises ~50 minutes later each day
- Tides follow the Moon
- Tide times advance ~50 minutes daily

**Practical Impact:**
- If HW is 1000 today, expect ~1050 tomorrow
- Some days have only 1 HW or 1 LW
- Explains why tide tables aren't the same daily

## Tidal Patterns by Region

**Semidiurnal** (2 equal HW/LW daily):
- UK waters
- US East Coast
- North Sea
- Most of Atlantic

**Diurnal** (1 HW/LW daily):
- Gulf of Mexico
- Some Pacific islands
- Java Sea

**Mixed Semidiurnal** (2 unequal HW/LW daily):
- US West Coast
- Philippines
- Parts of Australia
- Alaska

## Key Concepts

### Why Two Bulges?
1. **Near side**: Moon's gravity pulls water toward it
2. **Far side**: Earth pulled more than water (centrifugal effect)
3. Result: Two tidal bulges opposite each other
4. Earth rotates through both bulges = 2 tides per day

### Spring Tide Lag (Age of Tide)
- Springs don't occur ON new/full moon
- Lag 1-2 days (sometimes 3)
- Varies by location
- Ocean "sloshes" - takes time to respond

### Lunar Month
- 29.5 days (synodic month)
- New Moon to New Moon
- Two spring periods, two neap periods per month

## Common Misconceptions
- **"Spring tides in spring season"** â†’ NO! Springs = new/full moon (any season)
- **"Moon causes all tides"** â†’ Mostly, but Sun adds 46%
- **"Tides same time daily"** â†’ NO! Advance ~50 min due to lunar day
- **"Two tides everywhere"** â†’ NO! Some places have 1 or 4
- **"Springs occur ON full moon"** â†’ NO! Lag 1-2 days

## Memory Aids
- **"Full and New make big tides brew"** - Springs at full/new moon
- **"Quarter Moon, tide's a swoon"** - Neaps at quarters
- **"Fifty minutes late, that's the tidal trait"** - Daily 50-min advance
- **"Two bumps on Earth's waist, tides happen twice"** - Two bulges = 2 tides
