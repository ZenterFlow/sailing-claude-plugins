### Key Points for Passage Planning

1. **Timing lag**: Always add 1-2 days AFTER the moon phase
2. **Predictable**: Spring and neap tides alternate every ~7 days
3. **Check almanac**: Exact dates vary, always verify with tide tables
4. **Range matters**:
   - Spring tides = more water at HW, less at LW
   - Neap tides = moderate heights both HW and LW
5. **Spring â‰  Season**: "Spring" means "to leap" (larger range), not the season!

### Example Application
**Planning a passage over a shallow bar:**
- New moon on June 15th
- Spring tides expected June 16-17
- Best time to cross: June 16-17 at HIGH WATER (maximum depth)
- Avoid: June 16-17 at LOW WATER (minimum depth)
