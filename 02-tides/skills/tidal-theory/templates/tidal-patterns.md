## Tidal Pattern Types (Geographic Variation)

### 1. Semidiurnal Tides (Most Common)
**Two HIGH waters and two LOW waters per day of EQUAL height**

```
Height
  │     HW          HW
  │    ╱  ╲        ╱  ╲
  │   ╱    ╲      ╱    ╲
  │  ╱      ╲    ╱      ╲
  │ ╱        ╲  ╱        ╲
  │╱          ╲╱          ╲
  └───────────────────────────→ Time
  LW          LW         (24h 50min)
```

**Characteristics**:
- 2 high tides per lunar day (~24h 50min)
- 2 low tides per lunar day
- Both HWs approximately EQUAL height
- Both LWs approximately EQUAL height
- Period between tides: ~6h 12min

**Locations**:
- UK and most of Europe
- East Coast USA
- Caribbean
- Most of Atlantic Ocean

### 2. Diurnal Tides (Rare)
**One HIGH water and one LOW water per day**

```
Height
  │         HW
  │        ╱  ╲
  │       ╱    ╲
  │      ╱      ╲
  │     ╱        ╲
  │    ╱          ╲
  │   ╱            ╲
  └──────────────────────→ Time
  LW              (24h 50min)
```

**Characteristics**:
- Only 1 high tide per lunar day
- Only 1 low tide per lunar day
- Period between HW and LW: ~12h 25min
- Simplest pattern but rare

**Locations**:
- Parts of Gulf of Mexico
- Southeast Asia (some areas)
- Java Sea
- Gulf of Tonkin

### 3. Mixed Semidiurnal Tides
**Two HIGH waters and two LOW waters per day of UNEQUAL height**

```
Height
  │     HW1               HW2
  │    ╱  ╲             ╱╲
  │   ╱    ╲           ╱  ╲
  │  ╱      ╲    HW2' ╱    ╲
  │ ╱        ╲      ╱╱      ╲
  │╱          ╲    ╱         ╲
  │            ╲  ╱           ╲
  └─────────────╲╱─────────────→ Time
               LW1    LW2
        (unequal heights)
```

**Characteristics**:
- 2 high tides per lunar day (DIFFERENT heights)
- 2 low tides per lunar day (DIFFERENT heights)
- "Higher high water" (HHW) and "lower high water" (LHW)
- "Lower low water" (LLW) and "higher low water" (HLW)

**Locations**:
- West Coast USA (Pacific)
- Australia (Pacific coast)
- New Zealand
- Parts of Western Pacific

### Why Do Patterns Vary?

**Factors affecting tidal patterns:**

1. **Moon's declination** (angle above/below equator)
   - When Moon is over equator: more equal semidiurnal
   - When Moon is far north/south: more mixed pattern

2. **Basin shape and depth**
   - Narrow bays and estuaries modify patterns
   - Continental shelf width affects resonance

3. **Coriolis effect** (Earth's rotation)
   - Affects large ocean basins
   - Creates rotary tidal currents

4. **Local geography**
   - Funnel-shaped bays amplify ranges
   - Islands and straits create complex patterns

### Identifying Pattern from Tide Tables

**Example 1: Semidiurnal (Southampton)**
```
Date: 15 June
HW 0615  4.2m
LW 1230  1.8m
HW 1845  4.3m ← Similar to morning HW
LW 0030  1.7m ← Similar to previous LW
```
**Pattern**: Semidiurnal (equal heights)

**Example 2: Mixed Semidiurnal (San Francisco)**
```
Date: 15 June
HW 0430  1.8m ← Higher high
LW 1045  0.3m ← Lower low
HW 1620  1.4m ← Lower high
LW 2235  0.8m ← Higher low
```
**Pattern**: Mixed Semidiurnal (unequal heights)

**Example 3: Diurnal (Pensacola, Gulf of Mexico)**
```
Date: 15 June
HW 0920  0.4m
LW 2150  0.0m
(Only one HW and one LW per day)
```
**Pattern**: Diurnal

### Exam Tip
**To identify tidal pattern:**
1. Count number of HWs and LWs per day
2. Compare heights of successive HWs and LWs
3. If 2 HWs of similar height → Semidiurnal
4. If 2 HWs of different height → Mixed Semidiurnal
5. If only 1 HW and 1 LW → Diurnal
