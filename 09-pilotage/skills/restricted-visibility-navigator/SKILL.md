## Purpose
Provide safe navigation procedures for restricted visibility (fog, heavy rain, snow). Covers COLREGS sound signals, navigation techniques, equipment usage, and safety protocols for visibility <2nm.

## Activation Triggers
- "restricted visibility"
- "fog navigation"
- "poor visibility"
- "fog signals"
- "sound signals"
- "fog procedures"

## Behaviour
1. **Define visibility status**:
   - Good: >5nm
   - Moderate: 2-5nm
   - Restricted: <2nm (COLREGS Rule 19 applies)
   - Poor: <1nm
   - Very poor: <0.5nm

2. **Sound signal requirements** (COLREGS Rule 35):

   **Power vessel underway** (making way):
   - 1 prolonged blast every 2 minutes

   **Power vessel underway** (stopped, not making way):
   - 2 prolonged blasts every 2 minutes

   **Sailing vessel**:
   - 1 prolonged + 2 short every 2 minutes

   **At anchor**:
   - Rapid bell ringing for 5 seconds every minute
   - (Vessel >100m: bell forward, gong aft)

3. **Navigation procedures**:
   - Reduce speed to safe speed for conditions
   - Post lookouts (visual and audible)
   - Sound signals as required
   - Radar if available (guard mode, CPA/TCPA alarms)
   - GPS/chartplotter for position
   - Frequent position fixes
   - Consider heaving-to or anchoring

4. **Safety actions**:
   - All crew on deck, lifejackets
   - Navigation lights on
   - Radar reflector hoisted
   - VHF on (listen for traffic)
   - Prepare anchor for immediate use
   - Brief crew on fog procedures

## Teaching Notes
- Rule 19 (restricted visibility) overrides Rules 11-18 (conduct in sight)
- "Safe speed" means able to stop in time to avoid collision
- Sound signals are LEGAL REQUIREMENT, not optional
- Lookout must listen as well as look
- If in doubt, slow down or stop
