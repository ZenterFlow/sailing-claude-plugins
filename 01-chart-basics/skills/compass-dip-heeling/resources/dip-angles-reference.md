# Global Magnetic Dip Angles Reference

## Understanding Dip Angle Data

**Dip Angle (Inclination):**
- Angle between Earth's magnetic field lines and horizontal surface
- Measured from horizontal plane
- Positive (+): Field lines point downward (Northern Hemisphere)
- Negative (-): Field lines point downward from above (Southern Hemisphere)
- Zero (0°): Field lines horizontal (Magnetic Equator)

**Note:** Values are approximate and change slowly over time (secular variation). Data based on 2020 IGRF (International Geomagnetic Reference Field) model.

## Dip Angle Contours

### Isoclinic Lines (Lines of Equal Dip)

**Northern Hemisphere:**
```
+80° Isocline: ~65-75°N latitude (High Arctic)
+70° Isocline: ~55-65°N latitude (Northern Europe, Canada)
+60° Isocline: ~45-55°N latitude (Northern USA, Central Europe)
+50° Isocline: ~35-45°N latitude (Southern Europe, Northern Africa)
+40° Isocline: ~25-35°N latitude (Subtropics)
+30° Isocline: ~15-25°N latitude (Tropics)
+20° Isocline: ~5-15°N latitude (Near Equator)
+10° Isocline: ~0-10°N latitude (Equatorial)
```

**Southern Hemisphere:**
```
-10° Isocline: ~0-10°S latitude (Equatorial)
-20° Isocline: ~5-15°S latitude (Near Equator)
-30° Isocline: ~10-20°S latitude (Tropics)
-40° Isocline: ~20-30°S latitude (Subtropics)
-50° Isocline: ~25-35°S latitude (Southern Africa, Australia)
-60° Isocline: ~30-40°S latitude (Southern Australia, NZ)
-70° Isocline: ~40-50°S latitude (Southern Ocean approaches)
-80° Isocline: ~50-60°S latitude (Southern Ocean)
```

**Important:** These are approximate bands. Actual isoclinic lines are irregular and vary with longitude.

## Secular Variation (Dip Change Over Time)

**Important Note:**
Magnetic dip angles change slowly over decades (secular variation).

**Typical rates:**
- 0.1-0.5° per year at mid-latitudes
- Faster at high latitudes
- Slower near equator

**Practical Impact:**
- Dip angles in this reference accurate within ±1-2° for 2020-2030
- Compass balance zones remain valid for decades
- No need to update compass for secular variation in typical vessel lifetime
- Charts/navigation software may show updated values

**Example:**
London dip angle:
- 2000: +66.5°
- 2020: +67.0°
- 2040 (estimated): +67.5°

Change: ~0.5° over 20 years (negligible for compass balancing)
