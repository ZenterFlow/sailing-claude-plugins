### Common Exam Questions

**Q1: Why does the far side of Earth have high tide?**
**A**: Centrifugal force from Earth-Moon rotation pushes water away on far side, creating second bulge.

**Q2: Why is the Moon's effect stronger than the Sun's?**
**A**: Moon is 400Ã— closer. Gravitational force decreases with distanceÂ², so proximity matters more than mass.

**Q3: Why do tides advance 50 minutes per day?**
**A**: The Moon orbits Earth, so lunar day = 24h 50min. Earth must rotate extra 50 min to realign with Moon.

**Q4: What causes spring tides?**
**A**: Sun and Moon aligned (new/full moon), combining their gravitational forces. Maximum 1-2 days AFTER moon phase.

**Q5: What's the typical period between high tides?**
**A**: 12h 25min (half of 24h 50min lunar day) for semidiurnal tides.
